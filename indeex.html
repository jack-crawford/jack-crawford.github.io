ABOUT

Originally from Tulsa, Oklahoma, Jack attended Holland Hall from 2001 until 2017. (That's no typo, he went to the same school from Pre-K through the twelth grade.)

After graduating from Holland Hall, Jack attended Villanova University, where he received his B.S. in Computer Science. While at Villanova, Jack was the president of the Association for Computing Machinery, the station manager at WXVU 89.1 FM, and most importantly, the host of The Come Up on Friday nights.

As a junior at Villanova, Jack began working for threshold.world as an intern, becoming a full developer before his senior year and joining the company full-time after graduation. At threshold.world, he leads development on b.world, a platform for impact-oriented teams to manage projects, measure outcomes, and tell their stories. He's very proud of it.

In 2023, Jack let one of his college roommates talk him into starting a podcast where they would analyze seasons of the reality-competition show Survivor, appropriately titled Survivor Analytics. Now in its sixth season, Survivor Analytics has 17,000 followers on TikTok and counting, and multiple former Survivor contestants have appeared on the show, including Survivor: China winner Todd Herzog.

That same year, Jack began pursuing his Master's degree in Creative Writing and Literature. He plans to complete that degree in the spring of 2026.

Jack now lives in Washington D.C. with his incredible partner but without any dogs, because you can only be so lucky.

b.world

b.world is a project management platform for impact-oriented teams. One of Jack's favorite features is Insights, which gives users the power to capture qualitative data at any point in a project's life-cycle, then use those Insights in storytelling. Read more here.

Zettelkasten

A Node script and Azure OCR power this system that auto-parses my handwritten papers and notecards and files them into an Obsidian vault, with smart indexing and relationships.

Ipseity

Ipseity was my senior project at Villanova, a proof of concept for real-time customizable spatialized audio in teleconferencing. Watch a demo here.

tangle

tangle was a social network I built that allowed users to upload almost any medium of content, so long as it fit in a little rectangle.
