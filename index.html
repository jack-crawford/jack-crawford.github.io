ABOUT ME

I'm a proud alumnus of Villanova University, class of '21 with a B.S. in C.S. In my time there, I had the privilege of serving as the station manager at WXVU 89.1 FM and as co-president of the Villanova chapter of the ACM. Currently, I work at threshold.world, where I'm part of the wonderful team building b.world, a platform that enables impact-oriented teams to improve their impact and storytelling. Also, I love to bake, read and write, and you can occasionally find me crocheting or attempting to avoid killing my plants. I'm also a co-host on the podcast Survivor Analytics, where we discuss the strategies and statistics behind the game show Survivor. On top of all that, I'm currently pursuing a Master's in Creative Writing and Literature from Harvard University.

I've been writing code professionally since high school, ranging from mobile development to backend API creation, from the iBeacon protocol to data science in R. From entire social networks built from scratch to image-focused GANs in Jupyter, I've built all sorts of stuff. I've also taken several courses in Data Science from Johns Hopkins via Coursera. My senior capstone at Villanova was Ipseity, a tool demonstrating a proof-of-concept for real-time customized spatial audio in teleconferencing. At threshold.world, I'm working as a full-stack product engineer, leading product design and implementation for b.world.
